Flight Delay Propagation

Interactive network visualization · Jan–Nov 2025 BTS data

What you're seeing

Each circle is a US airport. Connecting lines show routes where a late aircraft at one airport caused the next flight to depart late — a propagation event. The bigger and redder the circle, the more that airport drives cascading delays.

View Modes

  • Risk Score — node color/size by avg delay severity per event
  • Volume — node size by total propagation event count
  • Cascade — simulate a delay shock spreading from a hub

Interactions

  • Hover airport → see all metrics
  • Click airport → open detail panel with cause breakdown
  • Cascade mode → select a seed, click Play Cascade
  • Top 10 list → click any row to zoom to that airport
  • Scroll / drag the map to explore

Thresholds

A flight is considered delayed if arrival or departure exceeds 15 minutes — the BTS standard. The 15-minute threshold filters gate holds and minor ATC micro-delays.
